## Research Overview

My research lies at the intersection of **signal processing** and **machine learning**, with a particular focus on extending these techniques to irregular domains such as graphs. I am also interested in applications to audio and music processing.

## Research Projects

### Graph Signal Processing & Neural Networks

Extending classical signal processing operations (such as convolutions and translations) to graph-structured data. This work enables the application of powerful convolutional neural network architectures to irregular domains, opening new possibilities for analyzing data with complex underlying structures.

### Automatic Speech Recognition for Extreme Vocals

Developing specialized ASR systems capable of transcribing extreme vocal styles (such as those found in metal music), where traditional speech recognition systems fail. This research combines source separation, robust acoustic modeling, and domain adaptation.

### Music Information Retrieval

Analyzing musical content through signal processing and machine learning, including rhythm analysis, lyrics intelligibility assessment, and audio feature extraction.
